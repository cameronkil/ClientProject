The Brookfield General Store

A historic landmark that is still operational in the modern day.


This venue is open to the public and provides both the combination of supermarket and cafe.

Situated just on the west side of Mount Cooth

The Pullenvale Hall

A historic venue that holds decades of love, smiles and laughter!


The pullenvale hall is host to many birthday and family celebrations as well as weddings and social gatherings for all ages.

Brookfield Showgrounds

The Showground are a centre of activity from carnivals to festivals and much more. Also the venue to the iconic 'Brookfield Show'.


Located just next to the general store and a couple hundred metre from the local primary school. The showgrounds are enjoyable for all demographics from young to old.

Upbringing of Brookfield

history

Believed to be named by Lucinda Brimblecombe. Around this time of developement in the 1860s, the surrounding trees and bushland became open to logging and various types of farming.

The first church was developed and built in 1869 and earlier the next year in 1870 a committee was formed to raise funds for more community venues.

Brookfield post office became operational on 12 August 1876.

In 1885 1 acre of land was reserved for a community hall to be built.

The brookfield cemetery was opened in 1886.

A larger piece of land was bought for a new and improved church to be built in 1892. The total cost for the church to built was a total of 220 pounds.

First brookfield show ever held was on 20 july 1910.

demographics

2016 census, Brookfield had a population of 3,524 people. The median brookfield age was 46 years of age, 8 years above the Australian median.

In the 2021 census, Brookfield had a population of 3,640 people.
